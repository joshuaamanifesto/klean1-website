Our Story

A Century of
Excellence

In 1892, master watchmaker Henri Lebrun founded Manifesto in Geneva with a single belief: that time, properly honoured, becomes art. Working from a modest atelier on the banks of Lake Geneva, Lebrun perfected a movement so precise it shocked the watchmaking establishment.

Over 130 years, Manifesto has remained fiercely independent — never acquired, never compromised. Every movement is still assembled by hand in our original Geneva manufactory, where the philosophy of Henri Lebrun lives in every tick.

Milestones in Time
1892
Henri Lebrun founds Manifesto in Geneva, introducing the calibre ML-01 — a movement accurate to 2 seconds per day.
1924
Manifesto unveils the Grand Perpetual, the first perpetual calendar movement to account for centennial leap years.
1968
Third-generation Pierre Lebrun introduces the revolutionary flying tourbillon with sapphire crystal bridges — a world first.
2012
The centennial Cosmos collection launches to worldwide acclaim, fusing meteorite dials with proprietary micro-rotor movements.

The Craft

Inside the Atelier

Our Geneva manufactory is where obsession becomes precision. 47 master craftsmen, each with decades of dedication, transform raw gold and steel into mechanisms so refined they seem to defy the laws of physics.

01
Movement Assembly
Each movement comprises between 280 and 650 individual components, assembled under 10x magnification by a single watchmaker over 4 to 8 weeks. No component touches a machine once the assembly begins.
02
Dial Creation
Our dials are painted, fired, and finished across seven stages. Grand feu enamel dials take up to three months — each one unique, bearing the microscopic fingerprints of its creator.
03
Case Finishing
From raw 18K gold to mirror polish, a Manifesto case passes through 23 distinct finishing operations. The transition between brushed and polished surfaces is executed by hand to within one-tenth of a millimetre.
04
Quality Control
Before leaving Geneva, every Manifesto timepiece undergoes 21 days of testing across temperature extremes, water pressure, and positional accuracy. We reject 12% of movements — they are restarted from scratch.
05
Bespoke Service
Our private atelier accepts 24 bespoke commissions each year. From meteorite dials to engraved portraits, each piece begins with a personal consultation in Geneva and culminates two years later.
06
Restoration
Manifesto watches from 1892 still run in our restoration department. We maintain the original tooling and sourcing records for every movement ever produced — a promise of perpetual care.
Our Materials

Only the Finest

Au
18K Gold
Sourced from certified ethical mines, our gold alloys are custom-blended for colour consistency and hardness across every collection.
◈
Sapphire Crystal
Anti-reflective sapphire on all surfaces. Harder than any material except diamond, our crystals offer total clarity for a lifetime.
⊕
Alligator Leather
Hand-stitched straps from Louisiana alligator, tanned in Millau, France — the leather capital of the world. Each strap takes 6 hours.
Ti
Grade 5 Titanium
For sport collections, aircraft-grade titanium provides extraordinary strength at 40% less weight than steel, with hypoallergenic properties.
✦
Meteorite Dials
Our Cosmos series features genuine Gibeon meteorite dials — 4-billion-year-old iron-nickel crystals cut to 0.6mm thickness. Each pattern is unique to the universe.
